# Moonwish Studios — From Zero to 8 Clients


Co-founded with <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/bobby-zhong/" target="_blank">Bobby</a> in 2022 as a legally formed web development studio based in Orange County. Bobby handled sales, we coded projects together. Shipped **8 production websites** for local businesses and generated **$5k+ in revenue** over 8 months.

**Role:** Co-founder & Web Developer (Oct 2022 – June 2023)
**Stack:** React, TypeScript, Next.js, Tailwind CSS, Supabase, Figma, Vercel, Git

---

## What I Did

- Built modern, mobile-responsive websites for OC businesses end to end — design through deployment
- Led the implementation of a **Figma design system** that cut design cycles to **3–4 days** and kept UI consistent across projects and between collaborators
- Managed deployment, version control, and collaboration through **Vercel**, **Git**, and **GitHub**
- Ran client relationships — regular meetings, project planning, contracts, invoicing, payments, and check-ins
- Scoped projects, set timelines, and handled scope changes when clients shifted direction mid-project
- Still supporting these clients today — when something breaks or needs updating, they reach out and I fix it

---

## What I Learned

- **Owning outcomes, not tasks.** No manager to escalate to — if a client was unhappy or a deadline was tight, it was on me to fix it.
- **Speed compounds.** The design system, the reusable stack, the templated contracts — every process investment paid off across all 8 projects.
- **Clients don't know what they want.** Learning to translate vague requests into concrete deliverables was harder than the code itself.
- **Business is not just building.** Contracts, invoicing, scope management, and saying no to feature creep — this is where most freelancers fail.

## What I Took Away

Moonwish taught me how to own outcomes, not just tasks. When you're the founder, there's no one to escalate to — you either solve the problem or lose the client. That mindset carried directly into my work at Bluon, where I now own a product end to end with the same sense of accountability.

It also taught me that speed compounds. The design system, the reusable stack, the templated contracts — every investment in process paid off across all 8 projects. I think about this constantly when building developer tooling and infrastructure now.
